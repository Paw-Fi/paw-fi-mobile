# Moneko Mobile — What It Does (and How It Compares)

Moneko is a budgeting + spending assistant designed to make “staying on top of money” feel lightweight.

Instead of making you do a lot of manual work up-front, Moneko focuses on:

- Fast capture (text, receipt photo, voice note, files)
- Budgets that behave like “pockets/envelopes”
- A clean split between **personal** and **household (joint)** finances
- Practical insights (what’s happening now + “what if” planning)
- A WhatsApp assistant that can do the above from chat

This doc is written for users and non-users. It explains the four main sections you see in the app (Overview, Recurring, Pockets, Insights) plus WhatsApp.

---

## The Four Main Areas (Bottom Tabs)

### 1) Overview — Your Money, at a Glance

Overview is the home hub. It’s where you:

- See your key cards (spending summary, net cashflow, charts, recent activity)
- Switch between **Personal** and **Household** mode
- Change currency
- Add a new transaction quickly (the AI “+” flows)

#### Quick-add (AI capture)

Moneko supports “just tell it what happened” capture:

- Type a message (e.g., “Coffee 4.50”, “Spent 32 on groceries yesterday”)
- Snap a receipt photo (camera)
- Attach files (photos, PDFs, spreadsheets) when supported
- Record a voice note (describe the purchase)

Moneko tries to extract:

- amount
- date
- category
- merchant/description
- and whether it’s an **expense** or **income**

Then it routes you into a review screen where you can confirm and edit before saving.

#### Personal vs Household mode

You can track:

- **Personal** finances (just you)
- **Household / Joint** finances (shared spending and budgets with other members)

In household mode, the dashboard becomes “group-aware” and the app can show shared context.

---

### 2) Recurring — Bills and Income That Repeat

Recurring is for the transactions you don’t want to re-enter every month:

- recurring expenses (rent, subscriptions, utilities)
- recurring income (salary, stipend)

You can:

- view recurring expenses vs recurring income
- add a new recurring item
- edit existing recurring items
- delete recurring items

Why it matters:

- Recurring items are the backbone of predictable monthly planning.
- They also power parts of the calendar and forecasting experiences across the app.

---

### 3) Pockets — Your Monthly Budget, Organized

Pockets is Moneko’s envelope-style budgeting area.

Think of pockets as “containers” for your monthly budget:

- Groceries
- Eating out
- Transport
- Fun
- Bills
- etc.

For each month, you can:

- set a total budget
- split that budget into pockets (percentages / allocations)
- see how much you’ve spent per pocket
- see what’s left
- adjust, then save

#### Practical monthly workflow

- Start with a total monthly budget.
- Copy last month’s budget if you want a quick starting point.
- Allocate across pockets.
- As spending comes in, pockets update so you can see where you’re on track vs overspending.

#### Uncategorized spending

If spending arrives without a clear pocket, the app can show an “uncategorized” view and help you assign categories to pockets so future transactions classify correctly.

---

### 4) Insights — “What’s happening?” and “What if?”

Insights focuses on understanding and decision support:

- Running balance / daily balance views (budget vs spent vs trend)
- Scenario planning (ask questions like “What happens if I … by [date]?”)

#### Scenario planning (AI)

You can ask a question in plain language and pick a target date.

Moneko streams back a structured answer and can save scenarios so you can revisit them.

## Extras You’ll Notice in Daily Use

### Home screen widgets (optional)

Moneko can show key budget/pocket information on your home screen and offer quick actions, like:

- jump straight into Pockets
- start a quick add (text or camera)
- configure what a widget shows (Personal vs a specific Household, and which currency)

### Multi-currency

If you spend in multiple currencies, you can switch your “current currency view” so the numbers you’re looking at stay consistent for budgeting and charts.

### Notifications (optional)

Moneko can prompt you to enable notifications so important updates (like household-related events) can reach you even when the app is closed.

## WhatsApp Integration — Moneko in Chat

Moneko includes a WhatsApp assistant powered by an AI model.

This lets you do the same core money tasks without opening the app:

### What you can do in WhatsApp

1) Add transactions (expense or income)

- “Spent 12 on lunch”
- “Got paid 2400 salary”
- “Coffee 4.50 yesterday”

Moneko will:

- infer category and date when it can
- ask clarifying questions when it’s ambiguous
- confirm before saving if needed

2) Send a receipt photo

- Send an image of a receipt (optionally with a caption)
- Moneko extracts line items / totals when possible
- Then asks you to confirm what to save

3) Send a voice note

- Describe the purchase in a voice message
- Moneko attempts to extract the key details (amount, category, date)
- If it can’t, it guides you to retry or type it in

4) Ask for summaries (with totals)

Examples:

- “Show today’s spending”
- “How much did I spend this month?”
- “What are my top categories?”

Moneko’s WhatsApp assistant is explicitly designed to include:

- a total amount for the requested range
- and how many items are included

5) Budgets + pockets from chat

You can ask:

- “Set my budget to 2000 this month”
- “Split it: 40% rent, 25% groceries, 10% transport…”
- “How are my pockets doing this month?”

Behind the scenes, the WhatsApp assistant can create/update:

- monthly budgets
- pockets/envelopes (with percentage splits)
- pocket allocations
- category → pocket mappings (so spending lands in the right place)

6) Charts and quick buttons

If you ask for a chart/graph, the assistant can generate a chart image link and send it.

When there are a few choices to pick from (like which household or which pocket), it can send **tappable quick-reply buttons** (up to 3 options at a time) to keep chat fast.

7) Commands (optional)

You can chat naturally, but there are also shortcut commands for common actions (like help, setting a budget, listing expenses, and envelope-related actions).

### Personal vs Household in WhatsApp

The assistant supports both:

- personal spending
- household/joint spending

If you mention “household/group”, it can ask which household you mean (if you have multiple), and then operate on that scope.

### Verification and subscription notes (WhatsApp)

To protect your account:

- WhatsApp usage requires verifying your WhatsApp number (there’s a “Start Verification” flow).
- The WhatsApp assistant can be gated by subscription (free-plan users may be asked to upgrade before using it).

## How Moneko Compares (YNAB, Monarch Money, etc.)

This is a “what we optimize for” comparison, not a judgment of which app is best.

### A quick comparison table

| What you care about | Moneko | YNAB-style apps | Monarch-style apps |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Getting transactions in | Built for fast capture (text/photo/voice + review) | Often more manual/routine-driven | Varies by product; often dashboard-first |
| Envelope/pocket budgeting | Core feature (“Pockets”) | Often core feature/philosophy | Sometimes secondary or optional |
| Household/joint finances | Built into the main flow | Varies | Varies |
| “What if?” planning | Built-in scenario planning | Typically more plan/budget workflow | Often broader “finance overview” planning |
| WhatsApp as a channel | Yes (capture, budgets, summaries, charts) | Not typical | Not typical |
| Home screen widgets | Yes (budget/pockets + quick actions) | Varies | Varies |

### Moneko vs YNAB (at a glance)

Moneko tends to fit you if you want:

- faster “capture-first” budgeting (AI helps with entry)
- envelope/pocket budgeting without heavy setup
- a household/joint mode built into the main experience
- budgeting from WhatsApp as an extra channel

YNAB tends to fit you if you want:

- a very method-driven system with a strong philosophy and routine
- hands-on control and deliberate planning as the core habit

### Moneko vs Monarch Money (at a glance)

Moneko tends to fit you if you want:

- budgeting + spending awareness centered around pockets/envelopes
- fast capture (text/photo/voice) and “AI helps me keep up”
- scenario planning and chat-first logging (WhatsApp)

Monarch-style apps tend to fit you if you want:

- a broad “personal finance dashboard” approach across categories like tracking, net worth, and planning (varies by product)

### What’s uniquely “Moneko”

- WhatsApp-native budgeting assistant (capture + budgets + summaries + charts)
- A mobile-first, month-based pockets flow that is designed to be easy to maintain
- AI capture that supports more than just typed entries (receipt photos, voice notes, and certain files)

## A Simple “Getting Started” Story

If you want to understand Moneko quickly, think of it like this:

1. Pick a mode: Personal or Household.
2. Pick your currency.
3. Set a monthly budget and split it into pockets.
4. Log spending the easy way (type, photo, voice, WhatsApp).
5. Use Insights when you want answers: “How am I doing?” or “What if I…?”

---

## What Moneko Is Not Trying to Be

Moneko is not meant to force you into a complicated workflow.

If a budgeting system only works when you have perfect discipline, it tends to break in real life. Moneko is built around “make it easy to keep going”:

- low-friction capture
- clear monthly pockets
- quick feedback on what changed
